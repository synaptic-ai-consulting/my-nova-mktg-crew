## Why This Matters

Technical brilliance doesn't secure budgets—business outcomes do. Many architects fail because they cannot explain the financial impact of their systems to leadership. To move from experimental pilots to enterprise production, you must speak the language of ROI.

---

## The Problem: Individual Gains ≠ Business Value

- 90% of software professionals now use AI tools
- 30–60% productivity gains reported in automated workflows
- 35–55% development velocity increases documented
- **The Gap:** Individual task optimization ≠ System-level performance
- Organizations struggle to translate speed into proportional business value

---

## Five Value Dimensions to Measure

| Dimension | What to Quantify |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Productivity** | Velocity gains, task completion rates, time-to-delivery |
| **Quality** | Error reduction, compliance adherence, customer satisfaction |
| **Scalability** | Cost per unit at scale, throughput capacity |
| **Risk Mitigation** | Fewer incidents, compliance violations, audit findings |
| **Strategy** | Revenue impact, market positioning, competitive advantage |

---

## Three-Layer Evaluation Framework

| Layer | What You Measure | Why It Matters |
|-------|------------------|----------------|
| **Model** | Accuracy, factual correctness, hallucination rates | Table stakes—baseline hygiene |
| **Agent** | Plan quality, tool selection accuracy, multi-step success | Differentiation—orchestration quality |
| **System** | End-to-end workflow completion, cost per resolution, business KPIs | Business value—what leadership cares about |

*Smart teams measure all three simultaneously.*

---

## ROI Formula: The Core Components

1. **Baseline (current state)** — Cost per unit, volume, quality metrics
2. **With agentic system** — New cost per unit, automation rate, quality metrics
3. **Savings** — Baseline cost − New cost
4. **Investment** — Development, infrastructure, operations
5. **Payback period** — Investment ÷ Monthly savings
6. **Year 2+ ROI** — No dev costs; operations only; savings compound

---

## Example: Customer Support ROI

**Baseline (Human-Only Support)**
- 24,000 queries/month
- Cost per resolution: $2.50
- Monthly cost: $60,000
- CSAT: 3.6 | FCR: 65%

**With Agentic System (70% automation)**
- 16,800 auto-resolved @ $0.05 each = $840
- 7,200 escalated @ $1.50 each = $10,800
- Monthly cost: $11,640
- CSAT: 4.1 | FCR: 70%

**Result:** $48K monthly savings • 81% cost reduction • 4 months to breakeven (on $200K investment)

---

## Beyond Cost: NPS & Revenue Impact

- NPS improvement: 32 → 42 (+10 points)
- 10-point NPS increase correlates to 5–10% revenue lift (industry dependent)
- For a bank with $50M customer book: 5% × $50M = **$2.5M additional revenue**
- 5-year impact: **$12.5M incremental revenue**

## Pitch to Stakeholders: Structure Your Business Case

1. **Executive summary** — One-line value proposition
2. **Baseline vs. target** — Side-by-side metrics
3. **Investment & payback** — Clear timeline
4. **Risk mitigation** — What could go wrong and how you'll handle it
5. **Success criteria** — How you'll know it's working
6. **Ask** — Budget, resources, approval

---

## What You Need to Log for ROI

For every agent decision, capture:
- **correlationId** — Trace entire customer journey
- **decision object** — Evaluate agent reasoning
- **cost** — Calculate ROI
- **complianceTag** — Filter for audit requirements

Without structured logging, you cannot prove ROI.
