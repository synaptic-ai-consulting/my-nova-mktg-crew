## Post Copy

Everyone's learning about AI agents. Almost nobody is shipping them.

The gap between "I completed the tutorial" and "It's running in production" is where most aspiring agentic architects stall. And it's not a knowledge gap — it's a deployment gap.

You've probably seen the pattern: build a prototype, demo it to your team, get a thumbs up, then... nothing. No production plan. No way to measure value. No roadmap from "cool demo" to "this runs our workflow."

McKinsey's latest State of AI report says 72% of organizations have adopted AI in at least one function — up from 55% a year ago. But the same report shows that only a fraction of those pilots make it past the proof-of-concept stage. The bottleneck isn't the technology. It's the execution layer between "this works on my laptop" and "this generates business value."

That execution layer is what separates an engineer from an architect.

I've been working through this problem for years — first at Cisco, now as an independent builder. What I've found is that most stalled pilots share the same three failure modes:

1. Wrong use case selection — high ambition, low visibility, no executive sponsor  
2. No phased roadmap — jumping from prototype to "full production" with nothing in between  
3. Tool proliferation — six frameworks, four models, three orchestrators, zero coherence

Each one is fixable. But you need a structured approach, not another YouTube walkthrough.

In 30 minutes you'll walk away with:

→ A scoring framework to pick your first project — low-risk, high-visibility  
→ A week-by-week 90-day roadmap from assessment to production deployment  
→ The specific traps to avoid so your pilot doesn't die in committee

Free. 30 minutes. No fluff — just the execution playbook.
