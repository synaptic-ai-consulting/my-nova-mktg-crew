## What’s working (conversion-positive)

### 1. Headline and hook are conversion-focused

- **New lead:** “Software development is moving from writing code to orchestrating agents. This course is what you need to stay relevant—and protect your career.”
- This puts **career relevance and urgency** above the fold, tied to a clear market shift (orchestration vs. writing code). It answers “why now?” and “why me?” immediately.

**Impact:** Strong improvement over a generic outcome line. Visitors who care about staying relevant will self-identify quickly → supports higher visitor→click.

### 2. Market authority and urgency in the body

- **Anthropic 2026 framing:** “In 2026, engineering value is shifting…”, “being a software engineer increasingly means orchestrating agents…”, “gap between early adopters and everyone else is widening fast.”
- **“Vibe coding” without structure** and **“keeps systems reliable and your role indispensable”** tie the offer to the Playbook and 5 signs positioning.

**Impact:** Credibility (Anthropic) + fear of falling behind (gap widening) + clear outcome (indispensable role) support both click and purchase.

### 3. Top 9 trending + cohort urgency

- **“Top 9 trending Engineering course on Maven this week.”** — Platform-level social proof is now on the page.
- **“(NOTE: Cohort 3 will be last one offered at the introductory price)”** — Clear reason to act now without being heavy-handed.

**Impact:** Both address earlier gaps; they should reinforce trust and nudge fence-sitters toward Enroll.

### 4. “Who this course is for” matches intent

- **Software developers and engineers** — “move from implementer to orchestrator.”
- **Tech leads and engineering managers** — “ship production-grade agent systems” and “already the ‘glue’ between teams and agents.”
- **AI/ML engineers and consultants** — “escape task-level work and sell enterprise-grade, orchestrated solutions.”

**Impact:** Mirrors the Playbook and 5 signs; visitors can see themselves in the bullets. Good for qualification and for reducing bounce among the right audience.

### 5. Instructor and “What you’ll learn” alignment

- **Instructor headline:** “Agentic Architect, Pearson SME, ex-Cisco” — reinforces the outcome (become an Agentic Architect) and credibility.
- **What you’ll learn intro:** “leave as an Agentic Architect who orchestrates agents” — consistent outcome language.

**Impact:** Coherent story from hero → value → instructor → outcomes; supports perceived credibility and conversion.

### 6. Differentiators and trust

- **Personalized 1:1 coaching** and **Maven Guarantee** (refund through second week) are unchanged and strong.
- **Price** $1,500 and **two cohorts** keep the offer clear and actionable.

**Impact:** Reduces friction and perceived risk at consideration and checkout.

## Optional refinements (not required for lift)

### 1. Urgency note formatting

- The line “(NOTE: Cohort 3 will be last one offered at the introductory price)” does the job. If Maven allows, a short banner or bold line (e.g. **Cohort 3 — last at introductory price.**) could make it slightly more visible without changing meaning.

### 2. CTA copy test

- “Enroll” is clear. If you run A/B tests later, “Save your spot” or “Enroll in Cohort 3” could be tested for visitor→click; current CTA is already strong.

### 3. Prerequisites

- “CursorAI (Pro recommended)” and “AWS access (recommended)” are already slightly softened; no change needed unless you want to test even lower-friction wording.
