### What we covered

The theme of this session was one I keep coming back to: the gap between *learning* agentic AI and *shipping* it.

72% of organizations have adopted AI in at least one function (McKinsey, 2024). Less than 20% of those pilots ever reach production. The bottleneck is not the model, not the framework, not the tooling. It's the execution layer — orchestration, governance, and rollout discipline.

Here is the framework we walked through:

**How the SDLC is changing**
Software development is going through four distinct patterns: from Vibe Coding (fast, ungoverned, prototype-only) → AI Augmented SDLC (AI tools assisting humans) → Agentic Architect SDLC (AI Agent Personas orchestrated by a human architect) → Agentic VDLC (continuous, autonomous value delivery). Most teams are stuck at Pattern 1. Production requires Pattern 3.

**Step 1 — Use Case Selection: The Scoring Framework**
Four criteria to score every candidate use case before you write a line of code: Business Visibility (30%), ROI Clarity (30%), Technical Feasibility (25%), Risk Profile (15%). Target score: 3.5+. If a use case scores high on excitement but low on ROI Clarity, that's how pilot purgatory starts.

**Step 2 — The 90-Day Rollout Roadmap**
Four phases designed to de-risk the rollout while building stakeholder confidence:
- Phase 1 (Weeks 1–3): Internal Validation — agents running in parallel with humans, 100% output review
- Phase 2 (Weeks 4–6): Soft Launch — 25% production traffic with safety nets and daily standups
- Phase 3 (Weeks 7–10): Ramping — gradual scale from 40% → 70% with parallel team training
- Phase 4 (Weeks 11–12): Stabilization — ops handoff, runbooks, SLOs, steady state

**Observability: how you prove value (and keep the budget)**
The Three-Layer Evaluation Framework: Model Performance (table stakes), Agent Orchestration Quality (your differentiation), System & Business Outcomes (what the CFO cares about). Most teams obsess over Layer 1. The architects who get budget for Round 2 measure all three.

**The 3 pilot killers**
Wrong use case (high excitement, no ROI visibility) → single-launch strategy (no phased plan, no safety nets) → tool proliferation (7 frameworks in the first sprint). Each one is avoidable with planning.

**Change management is 50% of your job**
Smart design does not equal successful adoption. Real production rollouts fail not because the system broke — but because the team bypassed it. The five moves: involve early, show the jobs agents will do first, de-risk with a soft launch, celebrate wins publicly, offer career pathways.
