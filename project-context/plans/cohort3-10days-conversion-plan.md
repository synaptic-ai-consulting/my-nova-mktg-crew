#### E. LL narrowcast post-mortem — why opens were great but clicks were terrible

| LL Session | Sent | Open % | Click % |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|
| Engineer Effective Context (Feb 4) | 46 | 45.7% | 0% |
| Build Scalable Multi-agent AI Systems (Jan 21) | 404 | 38.9% | 0.2% |

**Root causes:**
1. **Sent to signups, not attendees.** "Build Scalable Multi-agent AI Systems" had 463 signups but only 61 attended. 404 received the email — most of those are cold leads who just put their name on a list. Attended vs. non-attended intent is dramatically different.
2. **Promo code fatigue.** LL25 was offered live in the session, again in the post-session follow-up, and again in these narrowcasts. By the third mention, it no longer feels exclusive.
3. **Email angle was transactional, not personal.** "Exclusive 25% off (5 days left)" reads as a promotional email, not a follow-up from an instructor. Someone who attended your session and didn't enroll has a reason beyond price — a discount subject line doesn't address the real objection.
4. **Wrong timing.** These emails went out a week or more after the sessions. The highest-intent window is 24-48h after the LL ends. The LL Feb 18 follow-up at 40.3% / 3.1% click confirms this — recency is everything.

**For Cohort 4 (takeaways):**
- Segment by attendance, not signup. Only attendees go into the "warm LL alumni" narrowcast.
- Send the first LL follow-up email within 24h of the session ending — this is when intent is highest.
